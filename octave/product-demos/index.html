What We Mean By "Demo" ?

Before choosing tools, we need shared language. Five different things are currently called "demos," and each has a different owner, effort level, and job. Naming them prevents scope creep and tells us what belongs on a product page versus elsewhere.

Type / Profile Definition User Length Business Objective Build Effort
A — Zoomable Still High-res screenshots expanding to full-screen on click with a use-case caption. Instant Enforces visual reality and rapid layout comprehension. Very Low
B — Marketing Interactive Short guided click-through of one core workflow; 2–4 key moments. 30s - 2min Accelerates on-page engagement and top-of-funnel pipeline. Low - Medium
C — Self-Guided Exploration Non-linear, user-driven tour with a sidebar menu to jump between key modules. 2 - 5min Drives deep technical evaluation and self-serve product proof. Medium
D — Technical Deep Demo Comprehensive technical walkthroughs closer to training or documentation. 10 - 30min Answers post-sale technical validation and integration parameters. High
E — Overview Video Narrated high-level feature reels or executive talking-head videos. 1 - 3min Builds broad brand awareness and solutions introduction. Medium

Direct Scoping Boundaries

  • On-Page Website Project Scope: Restricted strictly to **Types A, B, and C** to maximize top-of-funnel pipeline and user comprehension on our high-traffic pages.
  • Technical Demos Excluded: **Type D** is not an on-page product hero asset. It resides in the post-sales/support domain and should only be linked, not embedded.
Standards & Optimization

Best Practices for B2B Demos

Placement & CTA Strategy

  • High Conversion Lift: Embedded interactive tours convert at 4–6x the rate of static screenshots and lift time-on-page by 2–3x. Demos or their launch CTAs must reside above the fold.
  • Direct Action CTAs: Use "Take a Tour" or "Explore" variants rather than soft "Learn More" copy.
  • Persistent Access: Deploy a persistent "View / Book a Demo" CTA across product paths to close immediate conversion gaps, regardless of immediate demo asset readiness.

Length & Structural Architecture

  • Impact-First Flows: Highest completion performance stems from 1–6 step flows built around 2–4 core "aha" moments. Avoid feature checklists; ensure quick visual value within 60 seconds.
  • Welcoming Context: Utilize a brief welcome modal to establish user context (leveraged by 72% of top-converting B2B demos).
  • Non-Linear Options: Supply technical evaluators with a sidebar checklist/menu to jump directly to specific features rather than forcing linear paths.

Gating & Conversion Logic

  • Ungated Page Experiences: Keep on-page product tours 100% free of gates. Capture contact information only after value is proven, or pass to the main site form container.
  • Decoupled Lead Handling: The demo acts purely as a visual canvas. Leads are processed via Octave’s native site forms and routed to Salesforce, preserving accurate campaign attribution.
  • Clear Destination Paths: Never dead-end. Chain final demo frames to active next steps, such as pricing, custom live booking, or related workspace features.

Technical & SEO Performance

  • Web Components Over Iframes: Standard iframe wraps carry heavy loading speeds. Benchmarks show Web Component integrations load ~4.5x faster (21ms vs 116ms).
  • Lazy-Loading Mandate: Force lazy-loading for any iframe elements. Protecting page speed and LCP scores is a strict requirement to guard organic rankings.
  • Dual Deployment: Deliver every experience as both a responsive on-page embed and a standalone layout URL stripped of global site navigation for target sales outreach.
Execution Framework

The Octave's Demo Strategy

Core Messaging Thesis

  • Lead with the Outcome, Not the Interface: Our systems are workflow-intensive, not simply visually slick. Demos must highlight the job-to-be-done first ("Saves X hours"), using the interface screen captures strictly as supporting evidence. High-quality product copy must precede demo implementation.

Phased Delivery Roadmap (Fidelity vs. Coverage)

  • 🟢 Phase 0 — Near-Universal, Low Lift (Immediate): Deploy lightweight native lightbox zoom components on in-product stills, paired with drag-to-pan and use-case captions. Add a persistent "View / Book a Demo" CTA across all pages. Requires zero third-party licensing fees.
  • 🟡 Phase 1 — Hero Products (Premium Walks): Embed rich Type B (walkthroughs) and Type C (non-linear explorer) interactive tours across the top 10–12 high-traffic products. Standalone links are simultaneously generated for direct sales and email distribution.
  • 🔴 Phase 2 — Selective Depth & Customization: Introduce campaign-specific, persona-focused, or localized demo variants only when justified by explicit pipeline opportunities. Repurpose core assets into sales decks and training courses.

Success Validation Framework

  • Primary Pipeline KPIs: Influenced pipeline value (demo touch to opportunity pipeline) and on-page form conversion rates.
  • Diagnostic Metrics: Demo initialization rates, drop-off per step, and standalone sharing link clicks.
  • Control Group Comparison: Maintain systematic comparative analytics (demo-enabled pages vs. flat pages) to prove bottom-line performance.

What This Tells Us

  • The Great Decoupling Equalizer: Our design path of decoupled conversion is the ultimate selector. Consensus's biggest enterprise advantages—native form routing, automated lead captures, and deep, role-specific buying committee alerts—are exactly the things we have chosen **not** to rely on the demo tool for on the core website.
  • Arcade & Supademo Win the Race: Because we bypass native lead processing, the selection criteria shifts entirely to rapid production, marketing-grade visual polish, and page weight. This rewards **Arcade** and **Supademo** directly for on-page product embeds.
  • Consensus Retains a Critical Role: Consensus is highly valuable, but not on our public product pages. It should be used for Type D detailed support demos and target, sales-led account outreach where its deep committee tracking metrics can shine.
Operational POV

Strategic Recommendations

  • Standardize Vocabulary: Adopt the Type A–E demo taxonomy across engineering, sales, and marketing teams to establish clear expectations and prevent project scope creep.
  • Launch Phase 0 Immediately: Build and ship the native zoomable-stills component with a persistent "Book a Demo" CTA. This de-risks the program by providing instant value with zero vendor fees.
  • Standardize on Arcade / Supademo for Phase 1: Select Arcade as our primary on-page hero walkthrough tool for its premium visual zoom and pan polish, with Supademo serving as a highly performant and economical alternative.
  • Reposition Consensus: Move Consensus off high-traffic product pages and focus its use strictly on specialized target account plays and technical Type D walkthroughs where its tracking has high ROI.
  • Enforce Embed Performance Guards: Establish page-loading performance as a hard gate. Require web components or lazy-loaded frames to protect organic SEO scores.
  • Verify the New Consensus Editor: Monitor Consensus’s upcoming editor release. If creation time drops significantly, re-evaluate its role for public marketing pages.
Business Enablement

Single Tool Integration GTM Strategy

To meet executive timelines without engineering bottlenecks, we recommend deploying a single, modular Contentful component framework. This allows product marketing to deploy and swap interactive tools in real time based on their respective deployment times.

GTM Timing Options Breakdown

  • Immediate Launch (1-3 Days): Deploy **Arcade** or **Supademo** walkthroughs instantly on core landing layers. Highly responsive and lightweight, authors can record and publish within hours.
  • Strategic Pipeline Launch (4-6 Weeks): Transition high-intent product lanes to **Consensus** video branching networks. Focuses on late-stage qualification and enterprise sales enablement.

Comparison of Top 2 GTM Fast-Launch Tools

Arcade (Top Contender)

  • Pro: High-end cinematic zooming, panning, and micro-animations capture user attention immediately.
  • Pro: Extremely rapid build times and native, optimized web component embeds.
  • Con: Deeper HTML data capture features are restricted to higher pricing tiers.

Supademo (Alternative Option)

  • Pro: Most cost-effective entry point with rapid, intuitive browser-based step generation.
  • Pro: Provides highly performant, lightweight page embeds protecting LCP scores.
  • Con: Lacks some of the refined cinematic pan/zoom movement styles found in Arcade.

Key Strategic Benefits

  • 100% Development Decoupling: Marketing updates, updates paths, and swaps copy inside the CMS without engineering code changes.
  • Maximum Performance Security: Lazy-loaded code architecture maintains PageSpeed scores and protects organic rank visibility.
  • Attribution Preservation: Parent-to-iframe parameter passing keeps tracking, UTM sources, and analytics logs intact.
