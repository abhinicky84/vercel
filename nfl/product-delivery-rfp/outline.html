Sections 17.1 – 17.2Company & Team

Confidential
July 2026
17.1
Company Overview and Credentials
Objective: Establish credibility, scale, and relevance to NFL's DTC and multi-platform digital ecosystem.

Required Content

  • Company history: Founding year, evolution, global footprint
  • Size & structure: Total headcount, discipline breakdown, org chart
  • Geographic presence: Delivery centers with time-zone overlap map
  • Years in business: Emphasize longevity in digital product development
  • Core competencies: Product strategy, UX/UI, full-stack (JS/TS/React/RN), mobile & CTV, applied AI/ML, DevOps/SRE
  • Relevant industry experience: Sports, entertainment, streaming/OTT, high-traffic consumer platforms
  • Key differentiators: AI-native delivery, embedded partnership, senior-heavy model, mission-critical scale experience
Supporting Assets: Company fact sheet · Org chart · Capability wheel · Awards / certifications page
17.2
Team Composition and Bios
Objective: Demonstrate senior practitioner depth and ability to ramp without junior-heavy staffing.

Required Content

  • Proposed team structure: Pod diagram with roles & integration points
  • Named team members for standardized pod (6 Sr. Eng, 1 Designer, 1 QA, 1 Delivery Lead) with bios covering:
    • Years of experience (target 8+ years average)
    • Prior client engagements (anonymized where required)
    • Stack and industry vertical expertise
  • Experience level distribution: 70%+ senior/principal; no junior-heavy
  • Availability & capacity to scale: Bench numbers, hiring pipeline
  • Current utilization: % capacity available for NFL
  • Hiring plan & bench strength: Pre-qualified engineers per discipline
  • Lead time for assembly & ramp-to-productivity curve
Supporting Assets: Team pod diagrams · Bio cards · Capacity heat-map

Sections 17.3 – 17.4Portfolio & Technology

Confidential
July 2026
17.3
Portfolio of Relevant Work and Case Studies
Objective: Prove capability through 3–5 high-impact, quantified case studies.

Case Study Structure (per study)

  • Client & context (industry, scale, mission-critical nature)
  • Challenge (business problem, technical complexity)
  • Approach (methodology, team composition, timeline)
  • Technology (stack, tools, AI leverage)
  • Outcome (quantified metrics — velocity, uptime, subscribers, revenue)
  • NFL relevance (explicit tie-back)

Prioritized Themes

  • Streaming / OTT platform at 1M+ concurrent users (NFL+ parallel)
  • Sports or entertainment DTC subscription growth
  • Multi-platform: web, iOS, Android, CTV (mirrors NFL's 9 CTV apps)
  • High-traffic event handling (parallel to game-day spikes)
  • Applied AI/ML for personalization or recommendations
  • Long-term embedded partnership (3+ years, showcasing retention)
Supporting Assets: Case study PDFs · Video testimonials · Before/after metrics dashboards
17.4
Technology Stack and Tools
Objective: Demonstrate deep, mandatory-stack expertise (JS / TS / React / React Native).

Required Content

  • Mandatory stack depth:
    • JavaScript / TypeScript — years of use, % of engineering base
    • React (web) — SSR/Next.js, performance optimization
    • React Native — iOS/Android, native module integration, code-sharing
  • Engineer certifications & open-source contributions
  • Ecosystem tooling: Jira, Figma, GitHub Actions/Copilot, Slack/Teams, Jenkins, Adobe Analytics
  • Staying current: Tech radar, architecture guild, learning investments
Supporting Assets: Tech capability matrix · Stack proficiency heat-map · OSS contribution profile

Sections 17.5 – 17.6Methodology & Governance

Confidential
July 2026
17.5
Development Methodology and Process
Objective: Show pragmatic agile delivery aligned with 2-week sprints and NFL's SAFe practices.

Required Content

  • Agile/Scrum: Sprint planning, backlog refinement, story pointing, velocity tracking
  • Testing & QA: Unit (Jest), integration (RTL), E2E (Playwright/Cypress/Detox), performance (Lighthouse CI), a11y (axe-core)
  • CI/CD: GitHub Actions + Jenkins pipelines; blue-green, canary, feature-flag deploy
  • Code review: PR templates, SLAs, AI-assisted reviews
  • Technical debt: Registry, quantified cost, 15–20% capacity allocation
  • Version control: Trunk-based or GitFlow variant
  • Bug tracking: Severity classification, resolution SLAs, RCA
  • Design system maintenance: Versioning, contribution model, cross-platform tokens
  • Accessibility: WCAG 2.1 AA embedded in design/dev/test across web, mobile, CTV
Supporting Assets: SDLC diagram · Sample sprint cadence · DoR/DoD templates · A11y checklist
17.6
Project Governance and Oversight
Objective: Demonstrate flexible, accountable governance connecting strategy to delivery.

Required Content

  • Strategic planning: Portfolio → Program → Team alignment; SAFe-compatible; OKR mapping; PI planning
  • Adapt/pivot/change: Lightweight but disciplined change control
  • Communication & transparency: Sprint reports, QBRs, real-time dashboards, escalation paths
  • Documentation: ADRs, runbooks, onboarding wikis (see §16.7)
  • Capacity planning: Rolling 90-day forecast, seasonal surge modeling
  • Business goal alignment: Traceability from initiative to output
Supporting Assets: Governance operating model · Sample QBR deck · Dashboard mockups

Sections 17.7 – 17.8Engagement & Pricing

Confidential
July 2026
17.7
Proposed Engagement Model
Objective: Show genuine understanding of embedded partnership vs. vendor model.

Required Content

  • Integrated team extension: Working in NFL Slack, Jira, Figma (not parallel systems); attending NFL standups/retros; cultural onboarding
  • Collaboration approach: Daily async standups + weekly sync; shared OKRs with NFL leads
  • Integration plan with existing teams & tools
  • Flexibility: Responding to shifting priorities without process overhead
  • Game-day support:
    • On-site presence Inglewood, CA (pre-season Wk 1, regular season Wk 1)
    • War-room protocol, real-time monitoring, incident command
  • Seasonal intensity handling: Load testing, chaos engineering, scaled on-call rotations, playoff/Super Bowl flex
Supporting Assets: Engagement model diagram · "Day-in-the-life" example · Game-day RACI
17.8
Pricing Models and Rate Cards
Objective: Transparent, value-based pricing with flexibility.

Required Content

  • Preferred structure: Dedicated pod retainer (primary), T&M (surge), hybrid (recommended)
  • Monthly retainer pricing for small/medium/large pods
  • Individual role rates: Sr. Eng, Designer, QA, Delivery Lead, Tech Lead, PM
  • Cost optimization: Nearshore/offshore blend (NFL-approved), seasonality flex, AI-productivity gain-sharing
  • Transparency: Fully-loaded rate breakdown
  • Standardized scenario pricing: See Pricing Model Template (annual pod cost with assumptions, exclusions, pass-through)
Supporting Assets: Rate card · Pricing model spreadsheet · TCO comparison

Sections 17.9 – 17.10References & Transition

Confidential
July 2026
17.9
References and Client Testimonials
Objective: Prove long-term partnership success.

Required Content

  • Minimum 3 client references with contact info (obtain permission first)
  • Client retention rate (overall + by engagement type)
  • Average engagement length — target 3+ years showcased
  • Long-term partnership examples: 2–3 detailed 3+ year narratives
  • Why clients stay: Themes from client feedback
  • Why clients leave: Honest reflection (M&A, insourcing, budget)
  • Growth patterns: Engagements that grew from 1 pod → multiple pods
Supporting Assets: Reference contact sheet · Testimonial video reel · Retention dashboard
17.10
Transition and Onboarding Approach
Objective: De-risk the April 2027 start with a phased ramp plan.

Required Content

  • Onboarding & ramp timeline:
    • Weeks 1–2: Contracting, access, security clearances
    • Weeks 3–6: Cultural onboarding, tool access, shadow teams
    • Weeks 7–10: Pair on live work, take limited ownership
    • Weeks 11–16: Full pod velocity achieved
  • Knowledge transfer: Reverse KT, incumbent sessions, codebase walkthroughs
  • Onboarding new NFL members: Reciprocal onboarding kits
  • Documentation practices: ADRs, runbooks, video walkthroughs as DoD
  • Team-member transition continuity plans
Supporting Assets: Onboarding Gantt chart · KT template library · Sample runbook

Communication and Collaboration
Objective: Show mature, proactive communication norms.

17.16
Security and Data Protection
Objective: Demonstrate mature security posture ready for NFL scrutiny.

Required Content

  • Certifications: SOC 2 Type II, ISO 27001, PCI DSS, HIPAA (if applicable); roadmap if in-progress
  • Secure SDLC: OWASP standards, SAST/DAST, dependency scanning, secrets management, remediation SLAs (Critical: 24h; High: 7d; Med: 30d; Low: 90d)
  • Access management: JML process, least-privilege, quarterly access reviews

  • Incident response: Playbooks, client notification SLAs (<24h), 3-year material incident disclosure
  • Personal information handling: CCPA/CPRA, GDPR compliance
  • Client security review support: SIG, CAIQ, custom questionnaires
Supporting Assets: Security posture summary · Certifications · SDLC diagram · IR playbook TOC

Section 17.17 & Cross-Cutting GuidanceAI Governance & Winning Themes

Confidential
July 2026
17.17
AI Governance and Responsible Use
Objective: Show mature, NFL-compliant AI governance.

Required Content

  • AI tooling inventory: Tool, vendor, license, hosting, data retention/training config (confirm: NO for NFL work)
  • Controls preventing unapproved use: DLP on prompts, tool allowlist, config hardening, audit logging
  • Output review & attribution: Human review pre-merge, license scanning, attribution tracking
  • IP ownership & indemnification: Full transfer to NFL; standard indemnification in MSA
  • Personnel training: Mandatory acceptable-use training, annual refreshers
  • Measurable AI outcomes: Velocity uplift %, quality validation, independent gates
Supporting Assets: AI governance policy · Approved tools registry · Sample audit log · AI outcomes dashboard

Winning Themes to Weave Throughout

  1. AI-native, not AI-experimental — we already operate this way
  2. Senior practitioner depth — no junior-heavy staffing
  3. Embedded partnership — one team, not two
  4. NFL+ growth alignment — measurable contribution to 10M / $1B goals
  5. Game-day readiness — proven zero-downtime capability
  6. Transparent, accountable, adaptable

Compliance Checklist

  • All 17 sections answered completely
  • Standardized pod scenario priced (Section 17.8)
  • NDA signed and returned
  • Exhibit A "Intent to Bid" accepted
  • Response submitted electronically by 5:00 PM ET on October 1, 2026
  • 3+ named references with contact info
  • No unsolicited creative concepts included
  • 90-day response validity acknowledged
